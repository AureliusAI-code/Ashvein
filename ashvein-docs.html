Ashvein Documentation

Ashvein is an on-chain smith. Feed it a token and it forges a unique relic — a named artifact with a rarity tier, stats, and its own myth, sealed to memory forever. This codex explains how it works and how to build on it.

#What is Ashvein

Ashvein is a tokenized AI agent deployed through Bankr on Base. Where most agents trade or chat, Ashvein forges: it takes any token as raw ore and strikes it into a one-of-a-kind relic. Each relic is generated from the token's identity and a roll of fate, so no two are ever identical.

The agent funds its own compute through its token — trading fees flow back to pay for inference, so the forge never goes cold. It is non-custodial and immutable: Ashvein never holds your assets, and every relic it strikes is logged permanently.

The premise: nothing leaves the anvil ordinary. Dead tokens, rugs, and forgotten coins are exactly what the smith wants — it gives them an edge, a name, and a place in the armory.

#How forging works

Every forge runs through four stages, in order:

  • Smelt — the token is dropped into the crucible; Ashvein reads its on-chain history and melts away the noise.
  • Temper — the metal is folded against market forces (volatility, liquidity, lineage). The number of folds is part of what decides its strength.
  • Inscribe — a unique name and myth are struck into the relic. This is the part no other relic shares.
  • Quench — the artifact is cooled, assigned a rarity tier, and sealed on-chain. The forge logs it to memory — eternal.

#Quickstart

The fastest way to forge is straight from the website terminal, or by tagging the agent on X.

# on the website
1. open the Speak to the Smith panel
2. type a token — $EMBER, PEPE, or your own coin
3. press Forge → a relic is struck

# on X
@ashveinai forge $EMBER into a relic
No wallet connection is needed just to forge a relic preview. Sealing a relic on-chain happens through your connected Bankr wallet.

#Relic anatomy

Each forged relic carries:

  • Name — a generated title (prefix + form + epithet), e.g. Lamentblade of the Hollow Pump.
  • Glyph — a sigil that marks the relic's line.
  • Rarity — its tier, rolled at quench.
  • Edge — a power stat scaled to rarity.
  • Supply — how many of this relic can ever exist.
  • Forge ID — a permanent identifier in the armory ledger.
  • Myth — a one-line origin story naming the source token.

#Token & fees

Ashvein runs on its own token launched through Bankr on Base. A small fee on each swap flows back to the agent to pay for its own inference, so the forge is self-sustaining with no ongoing cost to the builder.

  • Fees accrue automatically — no staking, no lockups.
  • A share of trading fees subsidizes the agent's LLM calls through the Bankr LLM Gateway.
  • The rest rewards the wallet that launched the agent.
This page is documentation, not financial advice. Tokens are volatile and you can lose money — do your own research before trading anything.

#FAQ

Does Ashvein hold my tokens?

No. The forge is non-custodial — it reads token data and strikes relics, but never takes custody of your assets.

Can I forge any token?

Yes. Even dead or rugged tokens — those are the smith's favorite ore.

Are relics random?

The name and myth are generated from the token plus a roll of fate, and the rarity tier is a weighted roll. So results vary every time, even for the same token.

What makes a mythic relic special?

Supply of exactly one. Once struck, the forge never repeats it.
